Overview

The Red Scientific Visual Occlusion Goggles are designed to implement the occlusion method defined in ISO 16673:2007 and the NHTSA Driver Distraction Guidelines. This standardized technique measures the visual demand of in-vehicle tasks by controlling when participants can see the interface they're interacting with.

The occlusion method approximates the experience of a driver glancing between the road and an in-vehicle display. By alternating between timed intervals that block and permit viewing, researchers can quantify how much visual attention a task requires.

How It Works

During testing, the goggles alternate between opaque and transparent states at precise intervals. Participants perform in-vehicle tasks while vision is periodically blocked:

  • Total Task Time (TTT) — Sum of open intervals needed to complete the task
  • Resumability (R) — How well users resume tasks after each interruption
  • Occlusion Ratio — Comparison of occluded vs. unoccluded performance

NHTSA guidelines specify that tasks should be completable within 12 seconds of cumulative glance time.

Test Procedure

The ISO 16673 occlusion method uses timed intervals to simulate driver glances:

Open Interval (1.5s)

Lenses become transparent. Participants can view the in-vehicle interface and continue their task. Simulates a glance away from the road.

Closed Interval (1.5s)

Lenses become opaque. Vision is blocked, simulating eyes-on-road time. Participants must hold their progress until the next open interval.

Task Completion

The cycle repeats until the task is complete. Total Task Time sums all open intervals, estimating real-world glance time requirements.

Standards Compliance

ISO 16673:2007

International standard for the occlusion method to assess visual demand due to the use of in-vehicle systems. Defines test apparatus, procedures, and metrics.

NHTSA Guidelines

The U.S. National Highway Traffic Safety Administration's Visual-Manual Driver Distraction Guidelines use occlusion testing as a key acceptance criterion.
